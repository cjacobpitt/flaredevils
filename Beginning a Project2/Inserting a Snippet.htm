Inserting a Snippet

 

Now that you know how to create snippets, you need to know how to be able to continuously add them into your project to save yourself time and energy. Since snippets are in their own files, you have to either insert them using the ribbon, or drag and drop them from the Content Explorer. This tutorial will show you both methods.

 

Inserting a snippet using the ribbon:

 

  1. Place your cursor in the area where you want to insert a snippet.

 

  1. Click on the Insert tab.

 

  1. Under the Single Source section, click Snippet. The Insert Snippet Link dialogue box appears.

 

  1. Click on your snippet.

 

  1. Click OK. The snippet appears in your project, and is marked with brackets.

 

Inserting a snippet using the drag and drop method:

 

  1. Go to the Content Explorer.

 

  1. Open the Resources folder.

 

  1. Drag your snippet into the area where you want it to be and drop it. The snippet appears in your project, and is marked with brackets.

 

Fill up:

If you made multiple paragraphs into a snippet, that is called a block snippet. In order to keep the format of your paragraphs consistent, you must insert the block snippet on a new line in the XML editor. If you insert a snippet into a line of text, that is called a text snippet.
