Creating an Output

 

An output is the format that you use when publishing your project. For example, a PDF would be an output type, or a website. Consider who your audience is, and the type of devices that they will be using to access your content when choosing an output. In order to produce an output, you need to build a target, which is essentially another word for an output. In this tutorial, you will learn how to build a PDF target, which will create a finished PDF version of your project.

 

  1. Open the Project Organizer.

 

  1. Open the Targets folder. Notice that the target you created at the beginning of your project (HTML5, meaning a web output) is listed as the primary target. We are going to have you create a new PDF target instead.

 

  1. Right click on the Targets folder.

 

  1. Click Add Target. The Add File dialogue box appears. Under File Type, make sure Target is listed.

 

  1. Under New from Template, leave the default template, which is MyTarget.

 

  1. Under File Name, give your target a name.

 

  1. Under Output Type, select PDF.

 

  1. Click Add. A menu appears with the name of your target. Here you can make adjustments to which text you are allowing to appear in your PDF output. There will be more on this later.

 

  1. Click on the Project tab in the ribbon.

 

  1. Click on the small arrow next to Build Primary. A drop down menu opens with a list of the targets in your project.

 

  1. Click the PDF target that you have just created and named. You are now building the PDF version of your project. A row appears in the section titled Builds, where you can see a bar indicating the progress of your PDF.

 

  1. Click on the row once the Status says Finished.

 

  1. Click View Output, located in the toolbar of the Builds section. This opens the completed PDF version of your project in another window.

 

Fill up:

If you want to build your primary target, meaning the HTML5 target that you picked originally, just click Build Primary then proceed with the same steps.
